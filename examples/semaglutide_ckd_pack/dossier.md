## Executive Summary
Evidence indicates that semaglutide is associated with a significantly lower risk of major adverse cardiovascular events in patients with type 2 diabetes and chronic kidney disease (CKD) or atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, without increasing the incidence of serious adverse events [40162642, 40156843]. In patients with non-diabetic CKD and overweight/obesity, semaglutide significantly reduced albuminuria [39455729]. Safety profiles remain consistent regardless of concomitant SGLT2 inhibitor use, although gastrointestinal adverse events are more frequently reported with semaglutide [40156843, 39455729].

## Clinical Interpretation
Evidence indicates that semaglutide is associated with a significantly lower risk of major adverse cardiovascular events in patients with type 2 diabetes and chronic kidney disease (CKD) or atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, without increasing the incidence of serious adverse events [40162642, 40156843]. In patients with non-diabetic CKD and overweight/obesity, semaglutide significantly reduced albuminuria [39455729]. Safety profiles remain consistent regardless of concomitant SGLT2 inhibitor use, although gastrointestinal adverse events are more frequently reported with semaglutide [40156843, 39455729]. These findings should be used for evidence review and education, not for individual diagnosis, emergency triage, or treatment instructions.

## Patient-Friendly Explanation
Research suggests that semaglutide may help protect the kidneys and reduce the risk of kidney failure or death in patients with chronic kidney disease [40316962, 38806171]. These findings indicate that the medication is generally safe and may slow the progression of kidney disease [40162642].
