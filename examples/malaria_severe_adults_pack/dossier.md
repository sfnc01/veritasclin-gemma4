## Executive Summary
For adults with severe or complicated malaria, intravenous artesunate is the treatment of choice, with intravenous quinine serving as the immediate alternative if artesunate is unavailable [26880088]. Patients with severe malaria should be managed in high-dependency or intensive care environments to address potential multi-organ failure and hemodynamic instability [26880088]. While some evidence from non-endemic cohorts suggests that isolated hyperparasitaemia may not be an independent risk factor for critical disease [40445171], other data indicate that parasitemia >5% may be a predictor of artesunate treatment failure [41077038].

## Clinical Interpretation
For adults with severe or complicated malaria, intravenous artesunate is the treatment of choice, with intravenous quinine serving as the immediate alternative if artesunate is unavailable [26880088]. Patients with severe malaria should be managed in high-dependency or intensive care environments to address potential multi-organ failure and hemodynamic instability [26880088]. While some evidence from non-endemic cohorts suggests that isolated hyperparasitaemia may not be an independent risk factor for critical disease [40445171], other data indicate that parasitemia >5% may be a predictor of artesunate treatment failure [41077038]. These findings should be used for evidence review and education, not for individual diagnosis, emergency triage, or treatment instructions.
