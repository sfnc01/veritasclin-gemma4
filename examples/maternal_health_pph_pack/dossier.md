## Executive Summary
Prevention of postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) focuses on the active management of the third stage of labor, with oxytocin identified as the gold standard for both prevention and initial treatment [27701915, 36345105]. Management strategies emphasize early diagnosis through measured blood loss, hemodynamic resuscitation, and a staged approach using uterotonics, uterine massage, and mechanical interventions like balloon tamponade [36345105, 37832561]. If pharmacological and conservative surgical measures fail, peripartum hysterectomy is considered the definitive life-saving procedure [27701915, 30578144].

## Patient-Friendly Explanation
To prevent and manage postpartum hemorrhage, healthcare providers use a combination of active management during birth and the use of specific medications to help the uterus contract [27701915, 32575159]. Research also emphasizes the importance of using standardized toolkits and clear protocols to ensure a rapid and coordinated response when heavy bleeding occurs [36345105].
